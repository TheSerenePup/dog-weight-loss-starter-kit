Practical steps that actually work (vet‑approved)

1. Start with portion reality

Use a dedicated measuring cup — not a random scoop. Most dog food bags overestimate portions. The included cup in a weight loss kit takes the math out of mealtime.

2. Slow down every meal

A slow feeder bowl can extend eating time from 30 seconds to 5+ minutes. This improves digestion and helps your dog feel fuller. Plus, it’s a simple brain game.

3. Swap, don’t starve

Use the treat guide to replace high‑calorie snacks with low‑calorie, high‑satisfaction options (like crunchy carrots or vet‑approved training bits).

4. Move with intention

The exercise plan in a good kit isn’t about marathon runs. It’s about consistency: sniff walks, gentle play, and short bursts that fit your real life.

What to avoid: “Diet” foods that are still calorie‑dense, and guessing portion sizes. Both keep you stuck.
